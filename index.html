History

Bacon was for centuries the staple meat of the western European peasantry. Varieties distinguished by cut of meat or curing process
became standardized in association with particular countries or regions, such as the Irish or various Italian styles.
The relatively long storage life of bacon made it the only br meat to gain importance as an international
trade commodity in the late 19th century.
